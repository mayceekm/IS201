Why I Love to Travel

Travel is one of my favorite activities because it allows me to get to know new places with the people I love, and I can create lasting memories with them and experience a different culture.

I plan on going to Mexico this summer on a study abroad and am excited to continue to grow in my Spanish speaking skills and connect with new people.

  1. It builds real cultural fluency. My Spanish minor is helpful in class, but nothing compares to using it in the field. When I've traveled to Spanish-speaking places, I've understood things about the language — and the people — that no textbook could teach.
    • Informal slang you only hear in local markets
    • The way tone and rhythm change by region
    • How people negotiate, joke, and connect
  2. It reframes your relationship with money. Seeing how people live in different economies — what things cost, what's prioritized, how wealth is distributed — makes finance feel far more urgent and human.
  3. It teaches discomfort tolerance. Getting lost, missing a train, ordering the wrong thing — these small failures are actually great practice for dealing with uncertainty. Finance demands that skill constantly.
  4. It shows you what "enough" looks like. Many of the most joyful people I've encountered while traveling have far less than I grew up with. That's a lesson worth sitting with.

My Travel Bucket List

These are the places I'm working my way toward — some for adventure, some for language practice, and some simply because I can't stop looking at pictures of them.

  1. Patagonia, Argentina & Chile — Remote, dramatic, and largely untouched. The end of the world feels like a good place to start something new.
  2. Morocco — The medinas, the architecture, the food, the color. I want to be completely overwhelmed by it.
  3. Japan — A country where ancient ritual and modern efficiency coexist without contradiction. That fascinates me endlessly.
  4. Portugal — Lisbon has been on my list since I started studying economics. It's a city that did a lot with a little, and bounced back from catastrophe more than once.
  5. Colombia — My Spanish and my curiosity about emerging markets collide perfectly here.
    • Medellín's transformation story is one of urban planning's most compelling cases
    • Cartagena's coastal history is layered and fascinating
    • The views look like something out of a painting
